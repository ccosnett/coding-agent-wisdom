# Notes

These notes sit behind the README.
They are not the guide itself.

## Working Themes

### 1. Plain language wins

Steinberger repeatedly argues against prompt theater.
The useful pattern is simple:

- say the task clearly
- give the right context
- ask for a visible result

Main source:

- [Just Talk To It - the no-bs Way of Agentic Engineering](https://steipete.me/posts/just-talk-to-it)

### 2. Good context beats vague instruction

The agent does better when it can work from real files, docs, screenshots, and failing commands.
This also appears in his codebase-understanding workflow and in his tooling around screenshots and readable documentation.

Main sources:

- [My AI Workflow for Understanding Any Codebase](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/understanding-codebases-with-ai-gemini-workflow)
- [Peekaboo MCP – lightning-fast macOS screenshots for AI agents](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/peekaboo-mcp-lightning-fast-macos-screenshots-for-ai-agents)
- [Peekaboo 2.0 – Free the CLI from its MCP shackles](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/peekaboo-2-freeing-the-cli-from-its-mcp-shackles)

### 3. The loop must stay fast

One of the clearest recurring ideas is that agentic work depends on fast feedback.
Rebuilds, tests, screenshots, and quick iteration matter because they reduce stale work and drift.

Main sources:

- [Shipping at Inference-Speed](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/shipping-at-inference-speed)
- [Poltergeist: The Ghost That Keeps Your Builds Fresh](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/poltergeist-ghost-keeps-builds-fresh)
- [The Future of Vibe Coding: Building with AI, Live and Unfiltered](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/the-future-of-vibe-coding)

### 4. Simple tools are better than brittle systems

Steinberger often prefers tools that are direct, CLI-friendly, and easy for both humans and agents to operate.
This appears in his writing on terminal workflows, MCP design, and remote terminal control.

Main sources:

- [MCP Best Practices](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/mcp-best-practices)
- [VibeTunnel: Turn Any Browser into Your Mac's Terminal](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/vibetunnel-turn-any-browser-into-your-mac-terminal)
- [Command your Claude Code Army, Reloaded](https://steipete.me/posts/command-your-claude-code-army-reloaded)

### 5. The human still has to judge

The posts are enthusiastic, but they do not remove the need for a responsible human.
The agent can search, draft, refactor, and test.
The human still has to decide what should be built, what is too risky, and what is good enough to accept.

Main sources:

- [Just Talk To It - the no-bs Way of Agentic Engineering](https://steipete.me/posts/just-talk-to-it)
- [My Current AI Dev Workflow](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/optimal-ai-development-workflow)
- [Claude Code is My Computer](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/claude-code-is-my-computer)

### 6. Tooling quality matters

If you build tools for agents, they should be treated like real software.
Clear descriptions, sensible defaults, recovery paths, logs, tests, and release discipline are not extras.

Main source:

- [MCP Best Practices](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/mcp-best-practices)

### 7. The pace has a cost

Steinberger is candid about the addictive side of this work.
The speed, novelty, and volume of ideas can distort judgment and consume more time than you notice.

Main sources:

- [Just One More Prompt](https://steipete.me/posts/just-one-more-prompt)
- [Stop Over-thinking AI Subscriptions](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/stop-overthinking-ai-subscriptions)
- [Self-Hosting AI Models After Claude's Usage Limits](https://steipete.me/posts/2025/self-hosting-ai-models)
